## Design Context

### Users
Public visitors evaluating Adrian's work, taste, and current experiments. The site needs to help them quickly understand what he builds, what is actively running, and where to go next without exposing private or internal-only details.

### Brand Personality
Operator-minded, warm, and precise. The voice should feel like a real personal system in public: confident without startup theater, technical without cosplay, and direct without feeling cold.

### Aesthetic Direction
Personal OS console. Warm graphite surfaces, restrained amber/sage signals, crisp mono typography, and page structures that feel like panes, prompts, logs, and process tables rather than generic marketing cards. Avoid glossy SaaS tropes, pastel gradients, and anything that feels like a template.

### Design Principles
1. Show live public context, not portfolio theater.
2. Use structure and typography to imply an operating system, not decorative gimmicks.
3. Keep navigation and shared data as the source of truth.
4. Make every public claim specific, modest, and safe to publish.
5. Preserve calm readability even when the interface feels technical.
