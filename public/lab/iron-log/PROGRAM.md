# 4-Week Strength & Longevity Program
**Built for:** Adrian Lumley | 150 lbs · ~13-14% BF  
**Goal:** Long-term physical capability, scapular rehab, maintained aesthetics  
**Split:** Push / Pull / Legs (3x/week) + Muay Thai Fridays  
**Equipment:** Dumbbells, cable machines (chest/back/legs), treadmill, Peloton, rower  

---

## Why Push / Pull / Legs

Upper/Lower gives more frequency per muscle but limits total volume per session. At 3 days/week with Muay Thai as a 4th day, **Push/Pull/Legs** is the better fit:

- Each muscle group gets direct work 1x/week + indirect stimulus from adjacent days
- Pull day becomes a natural scapular rehab session (face pulls, rows = rehab work)
- Legs day stands alone — no interference with scapular work
- Pairs well with Muay Thai (upper body fatigue spread across the week)

## 4-Week Progression

| Week | Theme       | RPE Target | Volume Change                     |
|------|-------------|------------|------------------------------------|
| 1    | Foundation  | 6–7        | Baseline sets/reps. Learn the pattern. |
| 2    | Build       | 7–8        | +2.5–5 lb on compounds. Add reps on isolation if easy. |
| 3    | Peak        | 8–9        | Highest effort. Push compounds. Top-end load. |
| 4    | Deload      | 5–6        | -40% volume. Same exercises. Recovery week. |

**Progression rule:** If you hit the top of the rep range for all sets with clean form, add 5 lb (compounds) or 2.5 lb (isolation) next week.

## Day A — Push (Monday)
*Chest, Shoulders, Triceps*

| Exercise                        | W1     | W2     | W3     | W4 (Deload) | Reps    | Rest    |
|---------------------------------|--------|--------|--------|-------------|---------|---------|
| DB Incline Press                | 4 sets | 4 sets | 4 sets | 3 sets      | 8–10    | 2–3 min |
| DB Flat Press (floor or bench)  | 3 sets | 3 sets | 3 sets | 2 sets      | 10–12   | 2 min   |
| Cable Chest Fly (Low-to-High)   | 3 sets | 3 sets | 3 sets | 2 sets      | 12–15   | 90 sec  |
| DB Seated Overhead Press        | 3 sets | 3 sets | 4 sets | 2 sets      | 10–12   | 2 min   |
| DB Lateral Raise                | 3 sets | 4 sets | 4 sets | 2 sets      | 15–20   | 60 sec  |
| Cable Tricep Pushdown (Rope)    | 3 sets | 3 sets | 3 sets | 2 sets      | 12–15   | 90 sec  |

**Coaching cues:**
- **DB Incline Press:** Slight arch, elbows 45–60° from torso. 3-second eccentric on every rep. Key movement — load it progressively.
- **DB Flat Press:** Full ROM. Touch chest, full lockout. No bouncing.
- **Cable Fly:** Keep soft elbow bend throughout. Squeeze at midline, slow return.
- **Overhead Press:** Brace core, don't flare ribs. Full overhead extension = serratus + scapular upward rotation practice. Good rehab overlap.
- **Lateral Raise:** Lead with elbow, not wrist. 3 seconds on the way down. Don't go past shoulder height.
- **Tricep Pushdown:** Elbows pinned. Spread rope at full extension for peak contraction.

*Then: Full Scapular Rehab Circuit (see below)*

## Day B — Pull (Wednesday)
*Back, Biceps — highest scapular rehab overlap*

| Exercise                        | W1     | W2     | W3     | W4 (Deload) | Reps        | Rest    |
|---------------------------------|--------|--------|--------|-------------|-------------|---------|
| Cable Lat Pulldown              | 4 sets | 4 sets | 4 sets | 3 sets      | 10–12       | 2–3 min |
| Seated Cable Row (Neutral Grip) | 3 sets | 4 sets | 4 sets | 2 sets      | 10–12       | 2 min   |
| DB Single-Arm Row               | 3 sets | 3 sets | 3 sets | 2 sets      | 10–12/side  | 90 sec  |
| Cable Face Pull                 | 3 sets | 3 sets | 4 sets | 2 sets      | 15–20       | 60 sec  |
| DB Incline Curl                 | 3 sets | 3 sets | 3 sets | 2 sets      | 10–12       | 90 sec  |
| Hammer Curl                     | 3 sets | 3 sets | 3 sets | 2 sets      | 12          | 60 sec  |

**Coaching cues:**
- **Lat Pulldown:** Lean back ~15°. Initiate with scapular depression BEFORE bending elbow. Pause at chest.
- **Seated Row:** Long stretch at the front — feel the rhomboids load up. Initiate by pinching shoulder blades, then pull elbows back.
- **Single-Arm Row:** Long ROM. Elbow goes back and slightly up. Squeeze the lat, don't rotate.
- **Face Pull:** This IS rehab work. Pull to forehead. External rotation at the end — elbows stay high and wide. Never go heavy here.
- **Incline Curl:** Arm behind body = long-head stretch. Supinate fully at top. No swinging.
- **Hammer Curl:** Neutral grip = brachialis focus. Adds arm thickness. Controlled tempo.

*Then: Full Scapular Rehab Circuit (see below)*

## Day C — Legs (Thursday)
*Quads, Hamstrings, Glutes, Core*

| Exercise                      | W1     | W2     | W3     | W4 (Deload) | Reps       | Rest    |
|-------------------------------|--------|--------|--------|-------------|------------|---------|
| DB Romanian Deadlift          | 4 sets | 4 sets | 4 sets | 3 sets      | 10–12      | 2–3 min |
| DB Goblet Squat               | 4 sets | 4 sets | 4 sets | 3 sets      | 10–12      | 2 min   |
| Leg Curl Machine              | 3 sets | 3 sets | 4 sets | 2 sets      | 12–15      | 90 sec  |
| DB Bulgarian Split Squat      | 3 sets | 3 sets | 3 sets | 2 sets      | 8–10/side  | 2 min   |
| Standing Calf Raise (DB)      | 4 sets | 4 sets | 4 sets | 3 sets      | 15–20      | 60 sec  |
| Cable Woodchop                | 3 sets | 3 sets | 3 sets | 2 sets      | 12/side    | 60 sec  |

**Coaching cues:**
- **DB Romanian Deadlift:** This is a hip hinge, not a squat. Push hips back, soft knee, neutral spine throughout. Feel the hamstring stretch. Biggest longevity investment in the program.
- **Goblet Squat:** Chest tall, elbows inside knees. Heel drive on the way up. Use Peloton shoes or heel elevation if ankle mobility is limited.
- **Leg Curl:** 3-second eccentric every rep. Toes slightly in = more bicep femoris activation. Don't let hips rise off pad.
- **Bulgarian Split Squat:** Front shin stays vertical. Drop straight down — this isn't a lunge. Best single-leg stimulus for glutes. Expect quad and glute DOMS.
- **Calf Raise:** Full stretch at the bottom (1-second pause). Calves respond to slow, high-rep work. Don't bounce.
- **Cable Woodchop:** Rotate from the hips, not the lumbar. Arms relatively straight. Core stays braced the entire time.

*Then: Full Scapular Rehab Circuit (see below)*

## Scapular Rehab Circuit
**Perform at the end of EVERY weights session.**  
Rest 45–60 seconds between exercises. This is maintenance work — not a burnout.

| Exercise             | Sets | Reps / Duration | Notes                                                       |
|----------------------|------|-----------------|-------------------------------------------------------------|
| Wall Slides          | 3    | 12              | Forearms on wall, slide up. Focus on upward rotation.       |
| Face Pulls           | 3    | 15              | External rotation at end. Elbows high. Light weight only.   |
| Y-T-W Raises         | 3    | 10 each         | Prone on incline bench or floor. Light DBs or bands only.   |
| Serratus Punches     | 3    | 15              | Key for winging. Punch forward + protract fully at top.     |
| Band Pull-Aparts     | 3    | 20              | Rear delt + rhomboid activation. Control the return.        |
| Dead Bugs            | 3    | 10/side         | Spine neutral, lower back pressed to floor. No rushing.     |

**Why this fixes scapular winging:**  
Winging = serratus anterior weakness + serratus not firing properly, often paired with tight pecs and weak rotator cuff. This circuit directly addresses all three: serratus punches load the serratus, face pulls + Y-T-Ws build rotator cuff and posterior shoulder, wall slides train upward rotation pattern. Consistency over 4–8 weeks makes a visible difference.

## Progression Notes — Week by Week

**Week 1:** Learn the movements. Don't go to failure. Leave 2–3 reps in reserve every set. Note your starting weights.

**Week 2:** On every compound movement (Incline Press, Lat Pulldown, Romanian Deadlift, Goblet Squat), add 5 lb if you hit the top of the rep range last week. On isolation movements, add reps or a small weight increase if they felt easy.

**Week 3:** This is the growth stimulus week. Push the big lifts. It's OK to get close to failure on the last 1–2 sets of compound movements. Don't grind reps with broken form. Stop a rep before form breaks.

**Week 4:** Active deload. Reduce total sets by ~40% and weight by ~20–30%. You're not resting — you're training with room to breathe. This is where the adaptations from Weeks 1–3 consolidate. Don't skip it.

---

*Next cycle: repeat the 4 weeks with slightly higher baseline weights. Every cycle you run, you accumulate more capability. That's the long game.*
